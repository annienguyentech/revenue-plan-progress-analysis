<h2 align="center">📌 Project Overview</h2>

<p>
The <strong>KPIM Mart Sales Revenue Progress Analysis</strong> dashboard provides management with a centralized view of revenue performance. It connects sales results with revenue plans and historical performance so users can quickly identify whether the business, stores, products, and customer groups are meeting expectations.
</p>

<p>
The report is organized into four interactive pages:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Overview:</strong> High-level revenue performance and year-over-year comparison</li>
  <li><strong>Store:</strong> Performance by district, manager, and individual store</li>
  <li><strong>Product:</strong> Revenue performance by category, subcategory, supplier, brand, and product</li>
  <li><strong>Customer:</strong> Revenue analysis by customer characteristics and promotion</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h2 align="center">🎯 Business Objectives</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Monitor actual revenue against planned revenue.</li>
  <li>Measure progress toward monthly and year-to-date revenue targets.</li>
  <li>Compare current revenue with prior-year revenue.</li>
  <li>Evaluate revenue growth across reporting periods.</li>
  <li>Identify top-performing stores, districts, and managers.</li>
  <li>Analyze product performance by category, supplier, brand, and individual product.</li>
  <li>Understand how customer segments and promotions contribute to revenue.</li>
  <li>Support faster, data-driven sales and operational decisions.</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h2 align="center">❓ Key Business Questions</h2>

<ol>
  <li>How much actual revenue has KPIM Mart generated?</li>
  <li>Is actual revenue meeting or exceeding the planned revenue target?</li>
  <li>How is the company performing on a year-to-date basis?</li>
  <li>How has revenue changed compared with the prior year?</li>
  <li>Which stores, districts, and managers generate the most revenue?</li>
  <li>Which stores are behind their revenue plans?</li>
  <li>Which product categories, brands, and suppliers contribute the most revenue?</li>
  <li>Which products have the strongest revenue plan progress?</li>
  <li>Which customer groups and occupations generate the most revenue?</li>
  <li>How does revenue growth vary across promotions and quarters?</li>
</ol>

<h2 align="center">🗂️ Data Model</h2>

<p>
The Power BI report uses a structured analytical model containing two fact tables, five dimension tables, and one dedicated measure table.
</p>

<table>
  <tr>
    <th>Table</th>
    <th>Type</th>
    <th>Purpose</th>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td><code>fact_sales</code></td>
    <td>Fact</td>
    <td>Stores detailed historical sales transactions and revenue activity.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td><code>fact_sales_plan</code></td>
    <td>Fact</td>
    <td>Stores planned revenue targets used for actual-versus-plan comparisons.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td><code>dim_date</code></td>
    <td>Dimension</td>
    <td>Supports year, quarter, month, and time-intelligence analysis.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td><code>dim_store</code></td>
    <td>Dimension</td>
    <td>Contains store, district, and manager information.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td><code>dim_product</code></td>
    <td>Dimension</td>
    <td>Contains product, category, subcategory, brand, and supplier information.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td><code>dim_customer</code></td>
    <td>Dimension</td>
    <td>Contains customer demographic, occupation, education, and loyalty information.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td><code>dim_promotion</code></td>
    <td>Dimension</td>
    <td>Contains promotion information for campaign-level analysis.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td><code>measure</code></td>
    <td>Measure</td>
    <td>Centralizes DAX measures used throughout the dashboard.</td>
  </tr>
</table>
